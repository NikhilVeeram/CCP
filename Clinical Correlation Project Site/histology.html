🔬 Histology of Acute Lymphoblastic Leukemia (ALL)

🩸 Scene 1: The Normal Landscape ▼
Before pathology begins, we must first understand what "normal" looks like. In a typical blood smear:
  • RBCs appear pink with central pallor (acidophilic, biconcave, anucleate).
  • Neutrophils are larger, with segmented nuclei and granular cytoplasm.
  • Lymphocytes are small with high N:C ratio and dense chromatin.
  • Platelets are tiny cell fragments involved in clotting.
🎨 Scene 2: The Power of Staining ▼
Special stains help differentiate immature leukemic cells:
  • Romanowsky stains highlight nuclear and cytoplasmic details; lymphoblasts appear large, with prominent nucleoli and a deep basophilic cytoplasm.
  • Diff-Quik, a rapid stain, brings out fine chromatin patterns and nuclear irregularities.
🔬 Scene 3: The Lymphoblast Revealed ▼
The hallmark of ALL: an overabundance of immature lymphoblasts. These large cells:
  • Have a high N:C ratio
  • Show fine chromatin and prominent nucleoli
  • Lack granules
Found in both peripheral blood and marrow.
🧫 Scene 4: Marrow Infiltration ▼
In ALL, the marrow is crowded with blasts — the architecture of healthy hematopoiesis is lost. Diagnosis requires >25% blasts.
🧬 Scene 5: Immunohistochemistry ▼
Cell surface markers pinpoint the type of ALL:
  • B-ALL: CD10, CD19, CD22
  • T-ALL: CD3, CD7
  • Immaturity Marker: TdT
⚙️ Scene 6: Diagnostic Tools ▼
Diagnosing Acute Lymphoblastic Leukemia isn’t based on one test alone — it’s a mosaic of methods.

From identifying abnormal cell counts to pinpointing genetic translocations and surface markers, each diagnostic tool plays a unique role in confirming the presence and subtype of ALL.
Technique Findings Example
Flow Cytometry Surface marker quantification (e.g., CD10+)
FISH BCR-ABL, t(12;21)
CBC + Diff ↑WBC, ↓RBC & Platelets
🧠 Scene 7: How It All Connects ▼

From peripheral smear to biopsy, staining to immunotyping — each technique reinforces the diagnosis:

Normal → Lymphocytosis → Smear: Blasts → Flow: CD10+ → Biopsy: >25% blasts → IHC: TdT+ → FISH: t(12;21) → Confirmed B-ALL
